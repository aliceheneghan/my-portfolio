"The history of the internet is more than just alpha nerds, brogrammers, and male garage-to-riches billionaires. Female visionaries have always been at the vanguard of technology and innovation."1

1918


The first known use of the word computer was in 1613 and it meant 'one that computes'2

1940


Part Hollywood star, part eccentric inventor, Hedy Lamarr met George Anthiel at a party in 1940 and the two would come up with the torpedo jamming frequency hopping technology that WiFi, GPS and Bluetooth evolved from.2


1991


The girls are here and grrr they are feisty. In 1991 Adelaide, Australia, a few of them got together to form the VNS Matrix collective and launched The Cyber Feminist Manifesto for the 21st Century that packed a punch! It was THE era of zines and manifestos. You can find out more about the whole movement here.

1992


Alongside the cyber feminists the Riot Grrrls were also kicking up dust. The 90s saw an explosion of punk rock girlbands such as Hole, Babes In Toyland and of course, Bikini Kill who published their manifesto in 1991 and released the iconic 'Rebel Girl' track in 1992. The movements were closely linked and the message was, make noise, make waves and DoItYourself! In a return to girlish self-esteem 3 and silliness, these women were taking up subculture space in a big way.
