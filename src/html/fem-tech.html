"The history of the internet is more than just alpha nerds, brogrammers, and male garage-to-riches billionaires. Female visionaries have always been at the vanguard of technology and innovation."1

1940


Part Hollywood star, part eccentric inventor, Hedy Lamarr met George Anthiel at a party in 1940 and the two would come up with the torpedo jamming frequency hopping technology that WiFi, GPS and Bluetooth evolved from.2


1991


The grrrls are and grrr they are feisty. In 1991 Adelaide, Australia, a few of them got together to form the VNS Matrix collective and launched The Cyber Feminist Manifesto for the 21st Century that packed a punch! It was THE era of zines and manifestos. You can find out more about the whole movement here.

1992


But the Computer Grrrls weren't the only ones getting busy, so were the Riot Grrrls. The band Bikini Kill published the Riot Grrrl manifesto in 1991 and released the iconic 'Rebel Girl' in 1992. Wanting anything but to be nice young ladies, these women were making noise and taking up not just cyberspace but subculture space in general!

I visited the Computer Grrrls Exhibition and it blew my socks off. So much so it inspired me to make this page 3 years later. 23 international artists let their imaginations off the leash in order to tackle History, Gender and Technology through art.
